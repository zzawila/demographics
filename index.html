Across 28 Predominantly Black NYC Neighborhoods, Only One has a Black-Owned Supermarket

Asian American-owned stores flourish in predominantly Asian neighborhoods

By Zachary Zawila
December 14, 2023

As increased scrutiny comes to the demographics of business owners and entrepreneurship opportunities available to disadvantaged communities, only one supermarket — of more than a thousand across the city — is owned by a Black entrepreneur.

In 28 majority-Black zip codes throughout New York, all large grocery stores but one are owned by chains or owners who are not Black. Meanwhile, in just three majority-Asian zip codes in the city, 27 Asian-owned supermarkets operate, with dozens more in neighborhoods with smaller Asian-American populations.

“Not all businesses are equally set up to succeed,” said Jonnel Doris, the former Commissioner of the NYC Department of Small Business Services, in a 2020 statement.

“While the Black community makes up 22 percent of New Yorkers, only 2 percent of businesses are Black-owned,” he said.

Still, the difficulties are not necessarily the fault of the business owners.

Redlining, loan discrimination, and other forms of business racism have presented hurdles to Black entrepreneurs as they try to start a business. Meanwhile, Asian-American immigrants tend to be wealthier, be better educated, and have more access to funding upon arriving in the United States than Black New Yorkers.

Two zip codes in Flushing and one in Glen Oaks, Queens, are predominantly Asian-American. Meanwhile, much of southern Queens and Brooklyn are predominantly Black, along with portions of northern Manhattan and the Bronx.
